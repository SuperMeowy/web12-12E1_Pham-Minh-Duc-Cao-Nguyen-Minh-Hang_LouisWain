New Acquisition: The Private Diaries of Richard Dadd

A rare glimpse into the mind of the Victorian fairy painter.

By The ФЭС Archive Team | October 15, 2025

The ФЭС Foundation is proud to announce the acquisition of the previously unexamined private diaries of Richard Dadd (1817-1886), the celebrated Victorian artist known for his meticulously detailed fairy paintings and his tragic personal history. This significant addition to our archives promises to offer unparalleled insights into the artist's creative process and his experiences with mental illness.

Unlocking a Creative Mind

Richard Dadd's life was marked by tragedy; in 1843, he murdered his father, believing him to be the Devil. He spent the remainder of his life in institutions such as Bethlem Royal Hospital and Broadmoor Hospital, where he continued to produce some of his most iconic works, including "The Fairy Feller's Master-Stroke."

These newly acquired diaries span several decades of Dadd's confinement, offering a unique first-person account of his daily life, his artistic inspirations, and his philosophical musings. Preliminary scans reveal not only textual entries but also numerous intricate sketches and symbolic annotations that appear to directly relate to his complex paintings.

Significance for Art History and Mental Health Studies

The diaries are expected to be invaluable for art historians seeking to understand the symbolic language within Dadd's work and his meticulous, almost obsessive, attention to detail. Furthermore, they provide a rare historical document for researchers in mental health, offering a window into the subjective experience of psychosis during the Victorian era.

"Dadd's diaries are a bridge, connecting the fantastical realms of his art to the stark reality of his confinement. They offer a profound human story that transcends time." — Dr. Eleanor Vance, Head Archivist, ФЭС Foundation

The Foundation will embark on a comprehensive transcription and digitization project for the diaries. Select excerpts and interpretations will be made available to the public in an upcoming exhibition, allowing a deeper appreciation for one of Britain's most fascinating and enigmatic artists.
